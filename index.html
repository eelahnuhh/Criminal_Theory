THEORY

What is Social Structure Theory?

Social structure theory suggests that a person's place in the socieconomic structure influences their chances of becoming a criminal. Poor people, for example, are more likely to commit crimes because they are unable to achieve social success and boost their social status in any other way. EXPLANATIONS

Three Branches of Social Structure Theory

Social Strain

Crime is a direct result of frustration and anger from low socioeconomic classes

Failure to achieve positively valued goals, disjunction of expectations and achievements

Each type of strain increases the chance that negative emotions will occur within an individual

"Strain Theory argues that crime occurs when there aren't enough legitimate opportunities for people to achieve the normal success goals of a society. In such a situation there is a 'strain' between the goals and the means to achieve those goals, and some people turn to crime in order to achieve success. (Thompson, 2023)"

Social Disorganization Theory

Communities with high population turnover rates, diverse populations, and poverty are more likely to experience high levels of crime due to weakened social bonds.

"Social disorganization is defined as an inability of community members to achieve shared values or to solve jointly experienced problems"

Cultural Deviance Theory

Combines effects of social disorganization and strain to explain how people who are living in lower class neighborhoods react to social isolation and economic issues

Walter Miller: staying within lower-class cultural norms increases the likelihood of joining in violent behavior or illegal behavior. Ex.: Being street smart may lead to drug deals.

CRIME

Features of Crime

Crime is an issue that plagues the lives of many all around the world. Many different factors contribute to why someone can and will commit a crime. Before taking this class, I believed the causes of crime were from someone just wanting to commit a crime just for the sake of it, greed, or the inability to provide for yourself and others around you.

After taking this course I now realize how much more goes into someone becoming a criminal. The areas you grow up in, the people around you, and opportunity all play a role in what you will become in the future.
